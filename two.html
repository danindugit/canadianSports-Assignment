Toronto Maple Leafs

Team Description

The Toronto Maple Leafs are a professional hockey team in the National Hockey League (NHL). Their slogan is HONOUR. PRIDE. COURAGE. Citizens of Toronto are very emotionally invested into this team, so if you're looking to fit in with the office crowd, or if you're just looking for a team that can get your heart beating at maximum capacity, the leafs are the team for you!

Team History

The Maple Leafs franchise was founded in Montreal on November 22, 1917. The Leafs have won an astounding 13 Stanley Cups, but their last championship was in the 1966-67 season. Since then, fans have been waiting long and hard for their next opputunity to hoist the trophy, but when being a fan of the Toronto Maple Leafs, patience is key...

All-time Greats:

  1. Johnny Bower

    2. Bower was fearless and aggressive goalie that led the leafs to 4 Stanley Cups. He mastered the poke check and was able to use it to have major impacts in games. Johnny Bower has been described as a "winner who refused to quit". He played a key role in the Leafs' most recent Stanley Cup championship in the 66-67 season.

  2. Dave Keon

    3. Dave Keon won the Calder Trophy in his rookie season. He played over 100 games in his hall-of-fame career. Keon ranks 3rd in all-time leafs offense. He was also known to be the best penalty killer of his time. Lastly, he played a key role in the Leafs winning 4 Stanley Cups.

  3. Darryl Sittler

    4. Darryl Sittler played 844 regular season NHL games. He was known for being an offensive powerhouse. To this day, Sarryl Sittler ranks 2nd in all-time highest-scoring leafs. In his 1975-76 season, Sittler scored 6 goals in one regular season game, and scored 5 in a playoff game that same year. He served a leadership role at the time since the Leafs had inadequate ownership and management.

  4. Tim Horton

    5. The iconic Tim Horton, who of course founded the fast food restaurant chain, Tim Horton's, is seen by many as the strongest NHL player of all time. Horton played 1185 games as a leaf. Each game was played with an defensive intensity that could not be matched. This led to his multple all-star apperances and three consecutive Stanley Cup wins.

Current Team

Currently, the Toronto Maple Leafs are playing in a all-Canadian division due to the travel restrictions of COVID-19. They are showing great potential, being the 1st place team in the Scotia North division. They are 41 games into the season with a record of 28-10-3, well ahead of the second-place Winnipeg Jets. This season will be exciting. With elite players in their prime such as Austin Matthews and Mitchell Marner, the Leafs are looking to be the first Canadian team to win a Stanley Cup since 1993.
